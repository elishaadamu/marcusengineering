Medical device development requires a profound understanding of both electronics and the specific standards set by regulatory bodies. At Marcus Engineering, we've consistently demonstrated our expertise in these areas.

Working alongside clients, we aid in defining requirements, ensuring the user experience aligns with safety considerations, and simultaneously developing a technical construction file that meets FDA requirements. Whether dealing with Premarket Notification (510k), Premarket Approval (PMA), or De Novo applications for Class 1, 2, or 3 devices, our guidance ensures alignment with regulatory expectations.

The pathway from design to prototype and then to delivery for medical devices is distinctly rigorous. It mandates a stringent adherence to FDA standards, far beyond traditional engineering processes. This adherence specifically calls for maintaining traceability between customer specifications and design results.

At Marcus Engineering, our R2E™ (Risk Reduction Engineering™) Design Controls, coupled with our sophisticated Hardware and Software development processes, aim to guarantee both functional and performance targets while addressing Regulatory and Safety needs.

For a more detailed look into our Hardware and Software development processes, view HERE.

Marcus Engineering efficiently addresses several regulatory guidelines, such as:

- ISO-13485 compliant R2E™ Design Controls
- Transitioning to manufacturing under ISO-13485
- ISO-14971 Risk assessments
- IEC-61000 Electromagnetic Compatibility
- IEC-61010-1 Medical Electronics Safety
- IEC-62304 Medical Device Software Safety

A core aspect of our approach is the R2E™ (Risk Reduction Engineering™) methodology. It emphasizes early risk minimization, clarity in processes, and addressing key requirements and design challenges early in the developmental phase. This approach is crucial in ensuring timely deliveries and fostering transparent communication.
